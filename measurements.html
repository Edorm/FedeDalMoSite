Your Perfect Fit

How to Take Your Measurements

Precision is the foundation of every garment. Follow this guide to capture your measurements accurately — so Federica can create something made entirely for you.

Before You Begin

A few essentials for accurate results

Taking your own measurements is straightforward when done correctly. For the most accurate results, wear form-fitting underwear or a thin base layer — avoid bulky clothing that adds volume. Stand naturally upright, without tensing your muscles or holding your breath.

Ideally, ask a trusted person to assist you: it is much easier to keep the tape measure level and taut when someone else is measuring. If you are measuring yourself, use a mirror to check alignment.

What you need: A soft fabric tape measure (not a metal one), a mirror, and a pencil to note down each figure. If you don't have a tape measure, a piece of string and a ruler will work as a substitute.

Step by Step

The Six Measurements

01
Height

Stand barefoot on a flat floor with your back against a wall. Keep your feet together, your head level, and your gaze forward. Have someone mark the wall at the top of your head, then measure from the floor to that point. Alternatively, use a door frame or a height chart.

Measure in the morning — we are slightly taller before the day's activities compress the spine.
02
Bust / Chest

Wrap the tape measure around the fullest part of your bust, keeping it parallel to the floor all the way around. The tape should lie flat against your body — snug but not tight. Make sure it passes across the widest point of your chest and across your shoulder blades at the back.

Breathe normally and measure at the end of a natural exhale for the most accurate reading.
03
Waist

Locate your natural waist — the narrowest point of your torso, usually about 2–3 cm above your navel. Wrap the tape measure around this point, keeping it level and parallel to the floor. The tape should be comfortably snug, not pulling in the skin.

Bend gently to one side — the crease that forms marks your natural waist precisely.
04
Hips

Stand with your feet together. Wrap the tape around the widest part of your hips and seat, typically about 20–23 cm below the natural waist. Ensure the tape is parallel to the floor and passes over the fullest point of the buttocks at the back. Keep the tape snug but not tight.

Check in a mirror from the side to confirm the tape is perfectly horizontal all the way around.
05
Shoulder Width

With a relaxed, natural posture, measure from the outer edge of one shoulder to the outer edge of the other — across the back. The starting and ending points are where the shoulder meets the arm (the shoulder seam point). Keep the tape straight across, not following the curve of the neck.

This measurement is best taken by a second person, as it is difficult to reach and hold the tape accurately on your own.
06
Inseam / Inside Leg

Stand with your feet slightly apart. Place the tape measure at the top of your inner thigh, right at the crotch seam, and measure straight down to the floor (or to your preferred trouser length). For most custom trousers, measuring to the ankle bone is a good reference point.

Wear the shoes you plan to wear with the garment for the most accurate hem length.
